Chapter 23 Substance Use, Social Context, and the Limits of Individual Choice: A Multi-Level ABM with Reinforcement Learning

The previous model treated mental health as emerging from stress accumulation across organizational layers. This one is harder to reduce to a single mechanism. Substance use initiation is shaped simultaneously by craving and neurobiological state, peer norms that shift what feels normal, neighborhood resource availability, and city-level policy — each operating on a different timescale. The model attempts to represent that structure honestly, which means accepting more moving parts in exchange for more plausible dynamics.

The key design choice is to embed Q-learning at every level of the hierarchy. Individuals do not simply react to their environment; they learn, however imperfectly, which behavioral strategies yield the most reward under their current constraints. Peer cliques update their norms based on observed group outcomes. Neighborhoods allocate outreach or policing based on aggregate use rates. City zones shift policy quarterly. That is a significant departure from the workplace model, where agents were largely passive recipients of institutional forces. Here, adaptation is happening at every scale.

23.1 Structure of the Hierarchy

The model runs four nested levels: CityZone at the top, Neighborhood below it, then Block and PeerClique at the meso level, and Individual at the base. The Block layer is structural — it holds cliques and passively drifts in retail exposure and visible disorder, but does not have its own Q-learner. Each other level does. Three city zones are assigned SES levels of 20, 50, and 80, roughly representing low, mid, and high socioeconomic context. Neighborhood and individual SES values inherit from their zone with added noise.

The temporal structure is non-uniform, which is worth noting. Individuals step every model tick (daily cadence). Cliques step every 7 ticks. Neighborhoods step every 30. City zones step every 90. That asymmetry is intentional. Peer norm shifts are slower than individual choices; policy adjustments are slower still. Running everything synchronously would collapse those differences and likely produce unrealistic dynamics.

23.2 The Q-Learning Core

All agents share the same QTable class, a minimal tabular Q-learner with epsilon-greedy exploration and exponential decay. The update rule follows the standard temporal-difference form:

\[ Q(s, a) \leftarrow Q(s, a) + \alpha \Big[ r + \gamma \max_{a'} Q(s', a') - Q(s, a) \Big] \]

where \(\alpha = 0.10\) is the learning rate, \(\gamma = 0.90\) the discount factor, and \(\epsilon\) decays from 0.30 toward 0.05. Continuous state variables are discretized into 5 bins before lookup, so the state space remains tractable even with multiple inputs.

def update(self, state, action, reward, next_state):
    td = reward + self.gamma * np.max(self.q[next_state]) - self.q[state][action]
    self.q[state][action] += self.alpha * td
    self.epsilon = max(self.epsilon_min, self.epsilon * self.epsilon_decay)

Dry run: at initialization, all Q-values are zero and the agent explores randomly. After a few dozen steps, the epsilon decay means roughly 70% of choices are greedy. Whether the learned policy is good depends entirely on whether the reward function encodes the right incentives — a limitation that applies to every level of this model.

23.3 Micro Level: Individual Behavioral Choice

Each individual has five possible actions: substance_use, prosocial_leisure, isolation, employment_search, and treatment_contact. The reward function is where the SES mechanism lives. Slow-payoff actions — employment search and treatment contact — are discounted proportionally to low SES, reflecting the empirical finding that resource scarcity compresses the subjective value of deferred rewards (Galea et al., 2020; Hatzenbuehler et al., 2013).

The individual state update after substance_use is representative:

\[ \text{craving}_{t+1} = \text{clip}(\text{craving}_t - 15 + \epsilon_t,\ 0,\ 100) \]

\[ \text{depression}_{t+1} = \text{clip}(\text{depression}_t + 4 + \eta_t,\ 0,\ 100) \]

The action provides immediate craving relief but nudges depression upward — a simplified version of the reward-sensitization / tolerance dynamic described in incentive salience theory (Berridge & Robinson, 1998). Background craving drift is always positive, representing withdrawal pressure:

# Background craving drift (withdrawal)
self.craving = np.clip(self.craving + np.random.normal(2, 1), 0, 100)

Dry run: a low-SES individual with craving at 70 will find substance_use more rewarding than employment_search in the short run, because the SES factor compresses the employment payoff to near zero. That is not irrational behavior; it reflects a structurally constrained reward landscape.

23.4 Meso Level: Peer Clique Norm Dynamics

Peer cliques update their descriptive norm for substance use (norm_use) and their inclusion threshold (inclusion) weekly, using a three-action Q-learner. The actions — normalize_use, promote_mutual_aid, and tighten_exclusion — adjust these norms incrementally and push them top-down to all members. The key mechanism is that norm_use enters directly into the individual reward for substance_use:

\[ r_{\text{sub}} = 8.0 \cdot \frac{\text{craving}}{100} + 5.0 \cdot \text{norm\_use} - 6.0 \cdot \text{policing} - 4.0 \cdot \frac{\text{depression}}{100} \]

That peer approval term — 5.0 * norm_use — is the social contagion mechanism. When a clique normalizes use, individual reward for that behavior rises, and the Q-learner is more likely to select it in future steps. That is consistent with descriptive norm theory (Cialdini et al., 1990) and with epidemiological evidence that peer substance use is among the strongest predictors of initiation.

def _apply_action(self, action_idx):
    action = CLIQUE_ACTIONS[action_idx]
    if action == "normalize_use":
        self.norm_use  = np.clip(self.norm_use  + 0.05, 0, 1)
        self.inclusion = np.clip(self.inclusion - 0.03, 0, 1)

Dry run: a clique in a low-SES zone that observes high use rates among its members may learn to normalize use because the reward for doing so (correlated with current use rate) is higher than the reward for promoting mutual aid, at least in early steps. Whether that reverses over time depends on whether depression accumulates enough to shift the reward landscape — a subtler dynamic that the model can in principle capture.

23.5 Neighborhood Level: Resource Allocation Policy

Neighborhoods operate monthly and face three actions: increase_outreach, increase_policing, and expand_harm_reduction. The reward function penalizes all interventions with a fixed cost of 5 and rewards reductions in use rate and improvements in safety, weighted by neighborhood SES. This is a rough approximation of the resource allocation tradeoffs studied in the harm reduction literature (Kerr et al., 2006).

A compact form of the harm-reduction reward is:

\[ r_{\text{HR}} = 7.0 \cdot \bar{u} - 2.0 \cdot \frac{\bar{d}}{100} - 5.0 \]

where \(\bar{u}\) is the average use rate and \(\bar{d}\) is average depression. The intervention is only worth choosing when use is already high — a potential feedback trap where harm-reduction resources arrive too late in low-resource neighborhoods.

def _reward(self, action_idx):
    if action == "expand_harm_reduction":
        return 7.0 * self.avg_use_rate - 2.0 * (self.avg_depression / 100) - cost

Dry run: early in the simulation, avg_use_rate is low because the model has not accumulated many substance_use actions yet. The harm-reduction reward is therefore near zero or negative, and the neighborhood Q-learner may initially prefer policing. Over 180 steps, that ordering may shift — or it may not, which is itself informative about how late-arriving harm-reduction signals fare against earlier policing lock-in.

23.6 Macro Level: City Zone Policy

City zones update quarterly — twice in a 180-step run. The four policy actions (restrict_licensing, improve_transit_access, expand_service_placement, increase_enforcement) affect structural variables that propagate downward. Transit access and service placement also nudge neighborhood SES upward via a small additive term, encoding the idea that infrastructure investment has cumulative equity effects:

\[ \text{ses\_mean}_{t+1} = \text{clip}\left(\text{ses\_mean}_t + 0.01 \cdot \frac{\text{transit} + \text{service}}{200} \cdot 10,\ 0,\ 100\right) \]

The zone mental health score is a composite:

\[ M_z = \text{clip}\Big(100 - \bar{d}_z + 0.20 \cdot \text{service\_placement} - 20 \cdot \bar{u}_z,\ 0,\ 100\Big) \]

self.mental_health_score = np.clip(
    100 - self.avg_depression
    + self.service_placement * 0.20
    - self.avg_use_rate * 20,
    0, 100
)

Dry run: a low-SES zone with avg_depression near 60 and persistent use will score poorly even if service placement improves, because the use-rate penalty dominates. That dynamic may suggest that zone-level policy alone cannot compensate for structurally embedded SES disadvantage — a plausible claim, though the model is far too stylized to treat as evidence.

23.7 Model Execution Order and Data Collection

The update schedule is worth being explicit about. Every step, all individuals are stepped directly. Clique, neighborhood, and zone steps are gated on modular time:

def step(self):
    self.current_step += 1
    for ind in self.individual_registry.values():
        ind.step()
    if self.current_step % 7 == 0:
        for clique in self.clique_registry.values():
            clique.step()
    if self.current_step % 30 == 0:
        for nb in self.neighborhood_registry.values():
            nb.step()
    if self.current_step % 90 == 0:
        for zone in self.zone_registry.values():
            zone.step()

This is a departure from the workplace model, where cascade happened strictly through the Mesa scheduler. Here the cascade is time-gated rather than hierarchically nested. The tradeoff is that bottom-up signals — say, an individual’s last action — are read by the clique on the next weekly step, not instantaneously. That introduces a small temporal lag into the feedback loop, which may actually be more realistic than synchronous aggregation.

The DataCollector tracks five model-level averages: substance use rate, stress, depression, craving, and social capital. Individual-level outcomes are accessed directly via get_individual_df() rather than through Mesa’s agent reporter, which keeps the collection logic simpler and avoids the Mesa 2.x agent reporter overhead.

23.8 Reading the Outputs

The most revealing output is likely the SES-stratified scatter of ses_score against substance_use_count. If the reward compression mechanism is working, lower SES agents should accumulate more substance use actions, not because they are modeled as intrinsically different, but because the action space is cheaper for them in expected reward terms. The Pearson correlation between SES and use count is computed directly and serves as a quick check on whether the gradient is in the expected direction.

The clique norm trajectories by zone are also worth examining closely. Low-SES zone cliques may converge toward higher norm_use values, which then reinforces individual substance use through the peer approval term. If that happens, it suggests the model is generating something like a descriptive norm trap — not designed in, but emerging from the reward structure at the clique level. The action distribution bar chart by SES tertile closes the picture, showing whether treatment_contact and employment_search are genuinely suppressed in low-SES agents or whether the epsilon-greedy exploration keeps those proportions roughly uniform.

23.9 What the Model Adds

The substantive claim this model makes is fairly specific: if SES compresses the relative value of recovery-oriented behaviors, and if peer norms adapt to observed use rates, then use initiation can become self-reinforcing in ways that are not reducible to individual risk factors. That is consistent with a structural view of addiction that runs from Bourgois’s ethnographic work through Galea’s population health models, though the formalization here is obviously much thinner than either.

The RL framing adds something that pure rule-based ABMs cannot: agents that adapt their strategies, and higher-level agents that adapt their policies. Whether that adaptation converges to anything meaningful in 180 steps is unclear — two quarterly zone updates is barely enough for the Q-learner to leave its initialization. A more careful analysis would run 500–1000 steps with repeated seeds and track whether the learned policies differ across SES zones. That remains the most important extension, and the current run should probably be read as a demonstration of structure rather than an analysis of behavior.

23.10 Extensions Worth Considering

Several modifications would strengthen the model without requiring architectural changes. Letting individual SES scores evolve — decreasing with sustained substance use, increasing with employment actions — would make the SES-use relationship bidirectional rather than fixed. Adding a stigma diffusion mechanism analogous to the support diffusion in the workplace model would allow treatment-seeking to be socially suppressed not just individually. At the neighborhood level, letting policing intensity reduce social capital (a well-documented empirical pattern in over-policed communities) would introduce a policy tradeoff that the current reward function ignores. Finally, separating craving from stress more explicitly — perhaps allowing craving to be modeled as a partially observable state — would bring the individual layer closer to computational accounts of substance use in the psychiatry literature.

    "substance_use",    # immediate craving relief, peer approval, risk of penalty
    "prosocial_leisure",# delayed reward, social capital gain
    "isolation",        # avoidance; short-term stress relief, long-term depression risk
    "employment_search",# long-term reward, low immediate payoff
    "treatment_contact",# delayed recovery, stigma cost
]

# Meso: clique weekly norm updates
CLIQUE_ACTIONS = [
    "normalize_use",        # increase descriptive norm for substance use
    "promote_mutual_aid",   # increase inclusion threshold for prosocial members
    "tighten_exclusion",    # exclude high-risk members to protect group
]

# Neighborhood: monthly resource allocation
NEIGHBORHOOD_ACTIONS = [
    "increase_outreach",
    "increase_policing",
    "expand_harm_reduction",
]

# City zone: quarterly policy shifts
ZONE_ACTIONS = [
    "restrict_licensing",
    "improve_transit_access",
    "expand_service_placement",
    "increase_enforcement",
]

# TABULAR Q-LEARNING HELPER
# ============================================================

class QTable:
    """
    Minimal tabular Q-learner.
    State is discretized externally before lookup.
    epsilon-greedy policy with decay.
    """
    def __init__(self, n_actions: int, alpha=0.10, gamma=0.90,
                 epsilon=0.30, epsilon_min=0.05, epsilon_decay=0.995):
        self.q: defaultdict = defaultdict(lambda: np.zeros(n_actions))
        self.n_actions   = n_actions
        self.alpha       = alpha
        self.gamma       = gamma
        self.epsilon     = epsilon
        self.epsilon_min = epsilon_min
        self.epsilon_decay = epsilon_decay

    def discretize(self, value: float, bins=5) -> int:
        """Map continuous [0,100] to integer bin."""
        return int(np.clip(value / 100 * bins, 0, bins - 1))

    def get_state(self, *continuous_vals) -> tuple:
        return tuple(self.discretize(v) for v in continuous_vals)

    def choose_action(self, state: tuple) -> int:
        if np.random.random() < self.epsilon:
            return np.random.randint(self.n_actions)
        return int(np.argmax(self.q[state]))

    def update(self, state, action, reward, next_state):
        td = reward + self.gamma * np.max(self.q[next_state]) - self.q[state][action]
        self.q[state][action] += self.alpha * td
        self.epsilon = max(self.epsilon_min, self.epsilon * self.epsilon_decay)

class Individual(Agent):
    """
    Individual agent at the micro level.

    State variables:
        craving, stress, depression, stigma, social_capital

    RL reward combines:
        + immediate craving/stress relief
        + peer approval (from clique norm)
        - withdrawal penalty (if substance_use skipped while craving high)
        - delayed mood penalty (depression worsens over time)
        - legal/social sanction (from neighborhood policing)

    SES modulation: low SES compresses the value of slow-payoff actions
    (employment_search, treatment_contact) relative to substance_use.
    This is the central mechanism from Galea et al. and the ScienceDirect
    reference in the brief.
    """

    def __init__(self, unique_id, model, clique_id, block_id,
                 neighborhood_id, zone_id, ses_score: float):
        super().__init__(unique_id, model)
        self.clique_id       = clique_id
        self.block_id        = block_id
        self.neighborhood_id = neighborhood_id
        self.zone_id         = zone_id
        self.ses_score       = ses_score          # 0 (low) – 100 (high)

        # State
        self.craving       = np.random.uniform(20, 60)
        self.stress        = np.random.uniform(30, 70)
        self.depression    = np.random.uniform(10, 40)
        self.stigma        = np.random.uniform(20, 50)
        self.social_capital= np.random.uniform(20, 60)

        # Behavior counters
        self.substance_use_count  = 0
        self.treatment_count      = 0
        self.last_action          = None

        # Q-learner: state = (craving_bin, stress_bin, clique_norm_bin)
        self.ql = QTable(n_actions=len(INDIVIDUAL_ACTIONS))

        # History
        self.stress_history    = [self.stress]
        self.depression_history= [self.depression]
        self.craving_history   = [self.craving]
        self.action_history    = []

        # Set by clique (top-down)
        self.clique_norm_use   = 0.5   # descriptive norm [0,1]
        self.clique_inclusion  = 0.5   # how inclusive the clique is
        self.neighborhood_harm_reduction = 0.0
        self.neighborhood_policing       = 0.5

    # ---- reward function ----------------------------------------
    def _compute_reward(self, action_idx: int) -> float:
        action = INDIVIDUAL_ACTIONS[action_idx]

        # SES discount on slow-payoff strategies
        ses_factor = self.ses_score / 100.0   # 0→1

        if action == "substance_use":
            r  =  8.0 * (self.craving / 100)          # immediate relief
            r +=  5.0 * self.clique_norm_use           # peer approval
            r -=  6.0 * self.neighborhood_policing     # sanction risk
            r -=  4.0 * (self.depression / 100)        # delayed mood penalty
            self.substance_use_count += 1

        elif action == "prosocial_leisure":
            r  =  4.0 * self.clique_inclusion          # social reward
            r +=  3.0 * ses_factor                     # only pays off if SES affords it
            r -=  2.0 * (self.craving / 100)           # craving still present

        elif action == "isolation":
            r  =  3.0 * (self.stress / 100)            # short-term stress relief
            r -=  5.0 * (1 - ses_factor)               # worse in low SES (no resources)

        elif action == "employment_search":
            r  =  7.0 * ses_factor                     # low in low-SES neighborhoods
            r -=  3.0 * (self.stress / 100)            # effortful
            r -=  2.0 * (self.craving / 100)

        elif action == "treatment_contact":
            r  =  6.0 * self.neighborhood_harm_reduction  # availability
            r -=  5.0 * (self.stigma / 100)               # stigma barrier
            r +=  4.0 * ses_factor                         # socioeconomic access
            self.treatment_count += 1

        return float(r)

    # ---- state dynamics ------------------------------------------
    def _apply_action(self, action_idx: int):
        action = INDIVIDUAL_ACTIONS[action_idx]
        self.last_action = action

        if action == "substance_use":
            self.craving    = np.clip(self.craving    - 15 + np.random.normal(0, 3), 0, 100)
            self.stress     = np.clip(self.stress     - 8  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)
            self.depression = np.clip(self.depression + 4  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)
            self.social_capital = np.clip(
                self.social_capital + 3 * self.clique_norm_use - 2, 0, 100)

        elif action == "prosocial_leisure":
            self.craving    = np.clip(self.craving    + 5  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)
            self.stress     = np.clip(self.stress     - 5  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)
            self.depression = np.clip(self.depression - 3  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)
            self.social_capital = np.clip(self.social_capital + 5, 0, 100)

        elif action == "isolation":
            self.craving    = np.clip(self.craving    + 8  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)
            self.stress     = np.clip(self.stress     - 3  + np.random.normal(0, 3), 0, 100)
            self.depression = np.clip(self.depression + 6  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)
            self.social_capital = np.clip(self.social_capital - 5, 0, 100)

        elif action == "employment_search":
            self.craving    = np.clip(self.craving    + 4, 0, 100)
            self.stress     = np.clip(self.stress     + 5  + np.random.normal(0, 3), 0, 100)
            self.depression = np.clip(self.depression - 2  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)

        elif action == "treatment_contact":
            self.craving    = np.clip(self.craving    - 10 + np.random.normal(0, 3), 0, 100)
            self.stress     = np.clip(self.stress     - 6  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)
            self.depression = np.clip(self.depression - 5  + np.random.normal(0, 2), 0, 100)
            self.stigma     = np.clip(self.stigma     + 3  + np.random.normal(0, 1), 0, 100)

        # background craving drift (withdrawal)
        self.craving = np.clip(self.craving + np.random.normal(2, 1), 0, 100)

    # ---- main step -----------------------------------------------
    def step(self):
        state = self.ql.get_state(self.craving, self.stress, self.clique_norm_use * 100)
        action_idx = self.ql.choose_action(state)
        reward = self._compute_reward(action_idx)
        self._apply_action(action_idx)
        next_state = self.ql.get_state(self.craving, self.stress, self.clique_norm_use * 100)
        self.ql.update(state, action_idx, reward, next_state)

        self.action_history.append(INDIVIDUAL_ACTIONS[action_idx])
        self.stress_history.append(self.stress)
        self.depression_history.append(self.depression)
        self.craving_history.append(self.craving)

# MESO LEVEL: Peer Clique
# Weekly update (every 7 model steps)
# ============================================================

class PeerClique(Agent):
    """
    Peer clique at meso level.

    Maintains descriptive norm for substance use and an inclusion
    threshold for prosocial members. RL adapts these norms based on
    observed aggregate outcomes of members.

    Bottom-up: receives average substance use rate and social capital.
    Top-down: pushes norm_use and inclusion back to individuals.
    """

    def __init__(self, unique_id, model, block_id, neighborhood_id, zone_id):
        super().__init__(unique_id, model)
        self.block_id        = block_id
        self.neighborhood_id = neighborhood_id
        self.zone_id         = zone_id
        self.members: list[Individual] = []

        self.norm_use       = np.random.uniform(0.3, 0.7)  # descriptive norm
        self.inclusion      = np.random.uniform(0.4, 0.8)  # mutual aid norm

        # Aggregated bottom-up signals
        self.avg_use_rate   = 0.0
        self.avg_social_cap = 0.0
        self.avg_depression = 0.0

        self.ql = QTable(n_actions=len(CLIQUE_ACTIONS))

        self.norm_history      = [self.norm_use]
        self.inclusion_history = [self.inclusion]

    def add_member(self, ind: Individual):
        self.members.append(ind)

    def _aggregate(self):
        if not self.members:
            return
        self.avg_use_rate   = np.mean([
            1 if m.last_action == "substance_use" else 0
            for m in self.members
        ])
        self.avg_social_cap = np.mean([m.social_capital for m in self.members])
        self.avg_depression = np.mean([m.depression     for m in self.members])

    def _reward(self, action_idx: int) -> float:
        action = CLIQUE_ACTIONS[action_idx]
        if action == "normalize_use":
            return 3.0 * self.avg_use_rate - 2.0 * (self.avg_depression / 100)
        elif action == "promote_mutual_aid":
            return 4.0 * (self.avg_social_cap / 100) - 1.5 * self.avg_use_rate
        elif action == "tighten_exclusion":
            return 2.0 * (1 - self.avg_use_rate) - 3.0 * (1 - self.inclusion)
        return 0.0

    def _apply_action(self, action_idx: int):
        action = CLIQUE_ACTIONS[action_idx]
        if action == "normalize_use":
            self.norm_use  = np.clip(self.norm_use  + 0.05, 0, 1)
            self.inclusion = np.clip(self.inclusion - 0.03, 0, 1)
        elif action == "promote_mutual_aid":
            self.inclusion = np.clip(self.inclusion + 0.05, 0, 1)
            self.norm_use  = np.clip(self.norm_use  - 0.02, 0, 1)
        elif action == "tighten_exclusion":
            self.inclusion = np.clip(self.inclusion - 0.05, 0, 1)

    def step(self):
        self._aggregate()
        state = self.ql.get_state(self.norm_use * 100, self.avg_depression)
        action_idx = self.ql.choose_action(state)
        reward = self._reward(action_idx)
        self._apply_action(action_idx)
        next_state = self.ql.get_state(self.norm_use * 100, self.avg_depression)
        self.ql.update(state, action_idx, reward, next_state)

        # push top-down to members
        for m in self.members:
            m.clique_norm_use  = self.norm_use
            m.clique_inclusion = self.inclusion

        self.norm_history.append(self.norm_use)
        self.inclusion_history.append(self.inclusion)

# BLOCK LEVEL: Structural intermediary
# Holds cliques; passes neighborhood signals down
# ============================================================

class Block(Agent):
    """
    City block — structural container between neighborhood and clique.
    No own RL; aggregates and relays signals.
    """

    def __init__(self, unique_id, model, neighborhood_id, zone_id):
        super().__init__(unique_id, model)
        self.neighborhood_id = neighborhood_id
        self.zone_id         = zone_id
        self.cliques: list[PeerClique] = []

        self.retail_exposure  = np.random.uniform(20, 60)  # substance outlet density
        self.visible_disorder = np.random.uniform(20, 60)
        self.perceived_safety = np.random.uniform(30, 70)

    def add_clique(self, clique: PeerClique):
        self.cliques.append(clique)

    def step(self):
        # passive drift in block environment
        self.retail_exposure  = np.clip(
            self.retail_exposure  + np.random.normal(0, 1), 0, 100)
        self.visible_disorder = np.clip(
            self.visible_disorder + np.random.normal(0, 1), 0, 100)
        self.perceived_safety = np.clip(
            self.perceived_safety + np.random.normal(0, 1), 0, 100)

# NEIGHBORHOOD LEVEL: Monthly resource allocation
# ============================================================

class Neighborhood(Agent):
    """
    Neighborhood agent.

    RL allocates outreach, policing intensity, and harm-reduction
    presence monthly (every 30 model steps). Reward is a composite
    of reduced aggregate substance use and improved safety perception
    net of cost.

    Pushes harm_reduction and policing signals down to individuals
    via block → clique chain.
    """

    def __init__(self, unique_id, model, zone_id, ses_mean: float):
        super().__init__(unique_id, model)
        self.zone_id  = zone_id
        self.ses_mean = ses_mean      # neighborhood average SES

        self.blocks: list[Block] = []

        self.outreach_level     = np.random.uniform(20, 60)
        self.policing_intensity = np.random.uniform(30, 70)
        self.harm_reduction     = np.random.uniform(10, 50)

        # Aggregated signals
        self.avg_use_rate   = 0.0
        self.avg_depression = 0.0
        self.avg_safety     = 0.0

        self.ql = QTable(n_actions=len(NEIGHBORHOOD_ACTIONS))

        self.harm_reduction_history = [self.harm_reduction]
        self.policing_history       = [self.policing_intensity]
        self.use_rate_history       = [self.avg_use_rate]

    def add_block(self, block: Block):
        self.blocks.append(block)

    def _get_all_individuals(self) -> list:
        inds = []
        for b in self.blocks:
            for c in b.cliques:
                inds.extend(c.members)
        return inds

    def _aggregate(self):
        inds = self._get_all_individuals()
        if inds:
            self.avg_use_rate   = np.mean([
                1 if i.last_action == "substance_use" else 0 for i in inds])
            self.avg_depression = np.mean([i.depression for i in inds])
            self.avg_safety     = np.mean([
                b.perceived_safety for b in self.blocks]) if self.blocks else 50.0

    def _reward(self, action_idx: int) -> float:
        action = NEIGHBORHOOD_ACTIONS[action_idx]
        cost = 5.0   # resource cost of any intervention
        if action == "increase_outreach":
            return 6.0 * (1 - self.avg_use_rate) - cost + 2.0 * (self.ses_mean / 100)
        elif action == "increase_policing":
            return 4.0 * (self.avg_safety / 100) - 3.0 * self.avg_use_rate - cost
        elif action == "expand_harm_reduction":
            return 7.0 * self.avg_use_rate - 2.0 * (self.avg_depression / 100) - cost
        return 0.0

    def _apply_action(self, action_idx: int):
        action = NEIGHBORHOOD_ACTIONS[action_idx]
        if action == "increase_outreach":
            self.outreach_level = np.clip(self.outreach_level + 5, 0, 100)
        elif action == "increase_policing":
            self.policing_intensity = np.clip(self.policing_intensity + 5, 0, 100)
        elif action == "expand_harm_reduction":
            self.harm_reduction = np.clip(self.harm_reduction + 5, 0, 100)

    def _push_top_down(self):
        for b in self.blocks:
            for c in b.cliques:
                for ind in c.members:
                    ind.neighborhood_harm_reduction = self.harm_reduction / 100.0
                    ind.neighborhood_policing       = self.policing_intensity / 100.0

    def step(self):
        # Step blocks (block-level passive drift)
        for b in self.blocks:
            b.step()

        self._aggregate()
        state = self.ql.get_state(self.avg_use_rate * 100, self.ses_mean)
        action_idx = self.ql.choose_action(state)
        reward = self._reward(action_idx)
        self._apply_action(action_idx)
        next_state = self.ql.get_state(self.avg_use_rate * 100, self.ses_mean)
        self.ql.update(state, action_idx, reward, next_state)

        self._push_top_down()

        self.harm_reduction_history.append(self.harm_reduction)
        self.policing_history.append(self.policing_intensity)
        self.use_rate_history.append(self.avg_use_rate)

# MACRO LEVEL: City Policy Zone
# Quarterly update (every 90 model steps)
# ============================================================

class CityZone(Agent):
    """
    City policy zone — highest level.

    RL shifts licensing, transit, and service placement policies
    quarterly. Reward favors reduced zone-wide use and depression
    net of implementation cost. Budget is constrained by zone SES.

    Top-down: pushes ses_policy_modifier to neighborhoods, which
    in turn modulates individual SES-based reward compression.
    """

    def __init__(self, unique_id, model, zone_ses: float):
        super().__init__(unique_id, model)
        self.zone_ses = zone_ses

        self.neighborhoods: list[Neighborhood] = []

        self.licensing_restriction = np.random.uniform(20, 60)
        self.transit_access        = np.random.uniform(30, 70)
        self.service_placement     = np.random.uniform(20, 50)
        self.enforcement_level     = np.random.uniform(30, 60)

        self.avg_use_rate   = 0.0
        self.avg_depression = 0.0
        self.mental_health_score = 50.0

        self.ql = QTable(n_actions=len(ZONE_ACTIONS))

        self.mh_score_history   = [self.mental_health_score]
        self.use_rate_history   = [self.avg_use_rate]

    def add_neighborhood(self, nb: Neighborhood):
        self.neighborhoods.append(nb)

    def _get_all_individuals(self) -> list:
        inds = []
        for nb in self.neighborhoods:
            inds.extend(nb._get_all_individuals())
        return inds

    def _aggregate(self):
        inds = self._get_all_individuals()
        if inds:
            self.avg_use_rate   = np.mean([
                1 if i.last_action == "substance_use" else 0 for i in inds])
            self.avg_depression = np.mean([i.depression for i in inds])
        self.mental_health_score = np.clip(
            100 - self.avg_depression
            + self.service_placement * 0.20
            - self.avg_use_rate * 20,
            0, 100
        )

    def _reward(self, action_idx: int) -> float:
        action = ZONE_ACTIONS[action_idx]
        cost = 4.0
        if action == "restrict_licensing":
            return 5.0 * (1 - self.avg_use_rate) - cost
        elif action == "improve_transit_access":
            return 4.0 * (self.zone_ses / 100) + 3.0 * (1 - self.avg_use_rate) - cost
        elif action == "expand_service_placement":
            return 6.0 * (self.avg_depression / 100) - cost
        elif action == "increase_enforcement":
            return 3.0 * (1 - self.avg_use_rate) - 2.0 * (self.zone_ses / 100) - cost
        return 0.0

    def _apply_action(self, action_idx: int):
        action = ZONE_ACTIONS[action_idx]
        if action == "restrict_licensing":
            self.licensing_restriction = np.clip(
                self.licensing_restriction + 5, 0, 100)
        elif action == "improve_transit_access":
            self.transit_access = np.clip(self.transit_access + 5, 0, 100)
        elif action == "expand_service_placement":
            self.service_placement = np.clip(self.service_placement + 5, 0, 100)
        elif action == "increase_enforcement":
            self.enforcement_level = np.clip(self.enforcement_level + 5, 0, 100)

    def _push_top_down(self):
        """
        Policy shifts propagate as SES modifier to neighborhoods.
        Better transit and service placement nudge effective SES upward.
        """
        ses_boost = (self.transit_access + self.service_placement) / 200 * 10
        for nb in self.neighborhoods:
            nb.ses_mean = np.clip(nb.ses_mean + ses_boost * 0.01, 0, 100)

    def step(self):
        for nb in self.neighborhoods:
            nb.step()
        self._aggregate()
        state = self.ql.get_state(self.avg_use_rate * 100, self.zone_ses)
        action_idx = self.ql.choose_action(state)
        reward = self._reward(action_idx)
        self._apply_action(action_idx)
        next_state = self.ql.get_state(self.avg_use_rate * 100, self.zone_ses)
        self.ql.update(state, action_idx, reward, next_state)
        self._push_top_down()

        self.mh_score_history.append(self.mental_health_score)
        self.use_rate_history.append(self.avg_use_rate)

class SubstanceUseModel(Model):
    """
    Multi-level ABM: Substance Use & Comorbid Distress under SES Stratification.

    Hierarchy (4 levels):
        Macro  — CityZone         (quarterly RL: licensing, transit, services)
        Upper  — Neighborhood     (monthly RL: outreach, policing, harm-reduction)
        Meso   — PeerClique       (weekly RL: norm_use, mutual aid)
                 Block            (structural container, passive drift)
        Micro  — Individual       (daily RL: 5-action behavioral choice)

    Update cadence:
        Daily  (every step):     Individual.step()
        Weekly (every 7 steps):  PeerClique.step()
        Monthly (every 30 steps): Neighborhood.step()
        Quarterly (every 90 steps): CityZone.step()

    SES stratification:
        zone_ses ∈ {low=20, mid=50, high=80} assigned per zone.
        Neighborhood SES inherits zone_ses ± noise.
        Individual SES inherits neighborhood SES ± noise.
    """

    def __init__(
        self,
        num_zones=3,
        num_neighborhoods_per_zone=2,
        num_blocks_per_neighborhood=2,
        num_cliques_per_block=2,
        num_individuals_per_clique=5,
    ):
        super().__init__()

        self.num_zones = num_zones
        self.num_neighborhoods_per_zone  = num_neighborhoods_per_zone
        self.num_blocks_per_neighborhood = num_blocks_per_neighborhood
        self.num_cliques_per_block       = num_cliques_per_block
        self.num_individuals_per_clique  = num_individuals_per_clique

        self.zone_registry:         OrderedDict[int, CityZone]    = OrderedDict()
        self.neighborhood_registry: OrderedDict[int, Neighborhood] = OrderedDict()
        self.block_registry:        OrderedDict[int, Block]        = OrderedDict()
        self.clique_registry:       OrderedDict[int, PeerClique]   = OrderedDict()
        self.individual_registry:   OrderedDict[int, Individual]   = OrderedDict()

        self.current_step = 0

        self._build_hierarchy()
        self._build_peer_network()

        # Only city zones registered; they cascade internally
        self.scheduler = RandomActivation(self)
        for zone in self.zone_registry.values():
            self.scheduler.add(zone)

        self.datacollector = DataCollector(
            model_reporters={
                "avg_substance_use_rate": lambda m: m._model_avg_use(),
                "avg_stress":             lambda m: m._model_avg("stress"),
                "avg_depression":         lambda m: m._model_avg("depression"),
                "avg_craving":            lambda m: m._model_avg("craving"),
                "avg_social_capital":     lambda m: m._model_avg("social_capital"),
            }
        )

    # ------------------------------------------------------------------
    def _ses_for_zone(self, zone_idx: int) -> float:
        """Assign zone SES: low / mid / high in order."""
        ses_levels = [20, 50, 80]
        return ses_levels[zone_idx % len(ses_levels)]

    def _build_hierarchy(self):
        ind_id   = 0
        clq_id   = 0
        blk_id   = 0
        nbh_id   = 0

        for z_idx in range(self.num_zones):
            zone_ses = self._ses_for_zone(z_idx)
            zone = CityZone(z_idx, self, zone_ses)
            self.zone_registry[z_idx] = zone

            for n_idx in range(self.num_neighborhoods_per_zone):
                ses_mean = np.clip(zone_ses + np.random.normal(0, 10), 0, 100)
                nb = Neighborhood(nbh_id, self, z_idx, ses_mean)
                self.neighborhood_registry[nbh_id] = nb
                zone.add_neighborhood(nb)

                for b_idx in range(self.num_blocks_per_neighborhood):
                    blk = Block(blk_id, self, nbh_id, z_idx)
                    self.block_registry[blk_id] = blk
                    nb.add_block(blk)

                    for c_idx in range(self.num_cliques_per_block):
                        clique = PeerClique(clq_id, self, blk_id, nbh_id, z_idx)
                        self.clique_registry[clq_id] = clique
                        blk.add_clique(clique)

                        for _ in range(self.num_individuals_per_clique):
                            ind_ses = np.clip(ses_mean + np.random.normal(0, 10), 0, 100)
                            ind = Individual(
                                ind_id, self,
                                clique_id=clq_id,
                                block_id=blk_id,
                                neighborhood_id=nbh_id,
                                zone_id=z_idx,
                                ses_score=ind_ses,
                            )
                            self.individual_registry[ind_id] = ind
                            clique.add_member(ind)
                            ind_id += 1

                        clq_id += 1
                    blk_id += 1
                nbh_id += 1

    def _build_peer_network(self):
        """
        Within-clique ties: p=0.70 (dense peer support)
        Cross-clique ties:  p=0.15 (organizational bridging)
        """
        for clique in self.clique_registry.values():
            ms = clique.members
            for i in range(len(ms)):
                for j in range(i + 1, len(ms)):
                    if np.random.random() < 0.70:
                        ms[i].connections = getattr(ms[i], "connections", [])
                        ms[j].connections = getattr(ms[j], "connections", [])
                        ms[i].connections.append(ms[j].unique_id)
                        ms[j].connections.append(ms[i].unique_id)

        all_inds = list(self.individual_registry.values())
        for i in range(len(all_inds)):
            for j in range(i + 1, len(all_inds)):
                if (all_inds[i].clique_id != all_inds[j].clique_id
                        and np.random.random() < 0.15):
                    all_inds[i].connections = getattr(all_inds[i], "connections", [])
                    all_inds[j].connections = getattr(all_inds[j], "connections", [])
                    all_inds[i].connections.append(all_inds[j].unique_id)
                    all_inds[j].connections.append(all_inds[i].unique_id)

    # ------------------------------------------------------------------
    def _model_avg(self, attr: str) -> float:
        inds = list(self.individual_registry.values())
        return float(np.mean([getattr(i, attr) for i in inds])) if inds else 0.0

    def _model_avg_use(self) -> float:
        inds = list(self.individual_registry.values())
        if not inds:
            return 0.0
        return float(np.mean([1 if i.last_action == "substance_use" else 0
                               for i in inds]))

    # ------------------------------------------------------------------
    def step(self):
        self.current_step += 1

        # Individual daily step (always)
        for ind in self.individual_registry.values():
            ind.step()

        # Clique weekly (every 7 steps)
        if self.current_step % 7 == 0:
            for clique in self.clique_registry.values():
                clique.step()

        # Neighborhood monthly (every 30 steps)
        if self.current_step % 30 == 0:
            for nb in self.neighborhood_registry.values():
                nb.step()

        # City zone quarterly (every 90 steps)
        if self.current_step % 90 == 0:
            for zone in self.zone_registry.values():
                zone.step()

        self.datacollector.collect(self)

    def run_model(self, n_steps: int):
        for _ in range(n_steps):
            self.step()

    # ------------------------------------------------------------------
    def get_individual_df(self) -> pd.DataFrame:
        rows = []
        for i in self.individual_registry.values():
            rows.append({
                "id":              i.unique_id,
                "zone_id":         i.zone_id,
                "neighborhood_id": i.neighborhood_id,
                "clique_id":       i.clique_id,
                "ses_score":       i.ses_score,
                "final_stress":    i.stress,
                "final_depression":i.depression,
                "final_craving":   i.craving,
                "final_social_cap":i.social_capital,
                "substance_use_count": i.substance_use_count,
                "treatment_count":     i.treatment_count,
                "avg_stress":      float(np.mean(i.stress_history)),
                "avg_depression":  float(np.mean(i.depression_history)),
                "avg_craving":     float(np.mean(i.craving_history)),
            })
        return pd.DataFrame(rows)

    def get_clique_df(self) -> pd.DataFrame:
        rows = []
        for c in self.clique_registry.values():
            rows.append({
                "clique_id":      c.unique_id,
                "block_id":       c.block_id,
                "neighborhood_id":c.neighborhood_id,
                "zone_id":        c.zone_id,
                "final_norm_use": c.norm_use,
                "final_inclusion":c.inclusion,
                "avg_norm_use":   float(np.mean(c.norm_history)),
            })
        return pd.DataFrame(rows)

    def get_neighborhood_df(self) -> pd.DataFrame:
        rows = []
        for nb in self.neighborhood_registry.values():
            rows.append({
                "neighborhood_id":   nb.unique_id,
                "zone_id":           nb.zone_id,
                "ses_mean":          nb.ses_mean,
                "final_harm_red":    nb.harm_reduction,
                "final_policing":    nb.policing_intensity,
                "final_use_rate":    nb.avg_use_rate,
                "avg_harm_red":      float(np.mean(nb.harm_reduction_history)),
            })
        return pd.DataFrame(rows)

    def get_zone_df(self) -> pd.DataFrame:
        rows = []
        for z in self.zone_registry.values():
            rows.append({
                "zone_id":           z.unique_id,
                "zone_ses":          z.zone_ses,
                "final_mh_score":    z.mental_health_score,

                "transit_access":    z.transit_access,
                "service_placement": z.service_placement,
                "enforcement":       z.enforcement_level,
                "avg_mh_score":      float(np.mean(z.mh_score_history)),
            })
        return pd.DataFrame(rows)
